Technical Specifications

Traditional Fabric Limitations and Health Concerns ‚ñº

Synthetic fabrics like polyamide (nylon) and polyester have long been the standard in performance wear. However, they come with a range of drawbacks that compromise user experience and health, making the need for innovative alternatives increasingly critical.

  • Allergic Reactions: Skin sensitivity and irritation risks affecting up to 30% of users
  • Poor Moisture Management: Can absorb sweat but lacks efficient transfer capability to surface
  • Health Concerns: High BPA content linked to serious cancer risks and hormonal disruption
  • Comfort Deficiency: Retains dampness and becomes increasingly heavy during extended wear

Studies have shown that polyamide fabrics, in particular, may contain high levels of bisphenol A (BPA), a chemical linked to serious health risks such as cancer, reproductive issues, and endocrine disruption. This poses significant concerns for athletes and fitness enthusiasts who engage in prolonged skin contact with these materials.

One of the primary limitations of traditional synthetic fabrics is their inability to efficiently manage moisture. While these fabrics can absorb sweat from the skin, they lack the ability to transfer that moisture to the surface for quick evaporation, resulting in discomfort, increased body temperature, and potential bacterial growth that leads to odor formation.
